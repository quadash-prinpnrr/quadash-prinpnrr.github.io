QuaDaSh

Quantification in the context of Dataset Shift

The QuaDaSh project is a 24-month research initiative funded by the Italian Ministry of University and Research (PRIN 2022 PNRR). It brings together a multidisciplinary team from the National Research Council (CNR), the University of Padova, and the University of Pisa to advance a specialized branch of Machine Learning known as Quantification.

Traditional AI often focuses on classifying individual items. However, many crucial fields—such as social sciences, epidemiology, and market research— are more interested in aggregate data, such as the total percentage of a population affected by a certain condition. Quantification is the task of accurately estimating these "prevalences" in unlabelled datasets.

The core challenge QuaDaSh addresses is Dataset Shift: the common discrepancy between the data used to train an AI model and the real-world data it encounters. The project aims to:

  • Build a Theoretical Framework: Design new protocols and algorithms to ensure quantification remains accurate even when data distributions change over time or across different environments.
  • Support Human Wellbeing: Apply these methods to critical social challenges, including measuring algorithmic fairness to prevent discrimination in AI, and improving poverty indicators through advanced statistical techniques (Small Area Estimation).
  • Environmental Impact: Enhance the accuracy of seabed cover mapping to better monitor marine ecosystems and prevent natural losses.

QuaDaSh is deeply committed to Open Science. The project will strengthen the international research ecosystem by enhancing QuaPy, a leading open-source software library, and by organizing global data challenges and workshops to foster collaboration and innovation. By improving how we analyze aggregate data, QuaDaSh provides essential tools for more evidence-based and equitable policy-making.
